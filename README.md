# THE THREE  WORDS NEVER SAID 
       
                                -kanishda

For fifteen years, everyone knew them as the inseparable pair.

Not lovers.

Not siblings.

Just two best friends.

He trusted her with everything. She knew every dream he had, every fear he hid, every scar he never spoke about. If she called him at 2 a.m., he would answer. If she needed help, he would drop everything. That was the kind of friend he was.

She loved him.

Not for a few months. Not because he was handsome or popular.

She loved him quietly for years.

She never confessed because she knew one truth that terrified her more than rejection.

One confession could destroy fifteen years of friendship.

She couldn't bear the thought of losing the one person who made her feel at home.

So she smiled.

She listened to him talk about football, exams, family, dreams, and life.

She carried a heart that grew heavier every day.

---

Both of them were twenty now, in their final year of college.

One evening she finally decided.

No more hiding.

She sent him a message.

"Can we meet at the park today?"

He replied almost instantly.

"Sure after football practice."

Football was his world. Nothing came before practice.

She smiled at the screen.

Maybe today her story would finally begin.

Or end.

---

On her way to the park, she walked past the college football ground.

The practice had just ended.

Students gathered near the field, cheering loudly.

She looked up.

A girl was standing in front of him holding a bouquet.

The whole team started shouting.

The girl confessed.

For a second the world became silent.

Then...

He smiled.

Without hesitation...

He said yes.

Cheers erupted across the field.

The two hugged while everyone clapped.

She stood there unnoticed.

No tears.

No scream.

Just a small smile.

Then she quietly turned around...

...and walked away.

---

She still went to the park.

Maybe out of habit.

Maybe because hope takes time to die.

She waited on the same bench until her phone vibrated.

"Busy today can't come."

No punctuation.

No explanation.

No emoji.

No call.

Just one plain message.

She stared at the screen for a long time before locking her phone.

Then she stood up...

and left.

---

The next morning he picked her up like always.

She got into the passenger seat with the same smile.

He looked happier than she'd ever seen him.

"I'm so happy right now."

She smiled.

"You know... my secret crush proposed to me yesterday."

"I always thought it would only happen in my dreams."

He laughed while talking about every tiny detail.

How nervous she looked.

How everyone cheered.

How perfect yesterday had been.

He never noticed she had become unusually quiet.

She simply turned toward the car window.

One silent tear slipped down her cheek.

He didn't see it.

He was too busy reliving the happiest moment of his life.

---

Months passed.

The two of them became the most talked-about couple on campus.

"The Lovebirds."

Everywhere they went, people smiled at them.

They looked perfect together.

His family adored the girl.

Their families shared the same social status.

Everyone celebrated the relationship.

She watched from a distance.

She was only a scholarship student.

Yet his parents had always treated her like another daughter.

Kindness made it even harder to hate anyone.

So she never did.

She simply disappeared a little more each day.

---

Graduation arrived.

Then engagement.

A few months later...

Wedding.

Life seemed determined to reward him with happiness.

Pictures filled social media.

Engagement rings.

Wedding outfits.

Family dinners.

Beach trips.

Anniversary celebrations.

Smiles in every photograph.

People commented,

"Made for each other."

"Soulmates."

"Perfect couple."

She pressed the like button once.

Then never again.

---

She stopped replying to friends.

Stopped attending reunions.

Stopped visiting places they once loved.

People assumed she had become busy.

No one knew she had simply become tired.

Silence became her closest companion.

But even silence felt unbearably loud whenever another picture of him appeared on her screen.

She never blocked him.

She couldn't.

She never unfollowed him.

She couldn't do that either.

She simply looked...

smiled...

and kept scrolling.

---

Years passed.

No one saw her in public anymore.

She quietly built a life far away from everyone who once knew her.

No dramatic goodbye.

No final confession.

No closure.

Just absence.

And strangely...

He never searched for her.

Perhaps he believed she was busy.

Perhaps he thought friendships naturally fade.

Or perhaps...

he never realized she had been carrying a heart he unknowingly broke.

---

Sometimes the greatest love stories are never written.

Not because they lacked love...

but because they lacked words.

She protected fifteen years of friendship with a silence that cost her everything.

And he lived happily...

never knowing someone had loved him enough...

to lose him without ever asking him to stay.
